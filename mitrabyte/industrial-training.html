1 IT / Internship / SIWES

Student Industrial Work Experience Scheme (SIWES) popularly known as Industrial Training [IT] is a program specially structured for undergraduates to allow them the practical work experience they are likely to meet in the labor market after they have graduated from school.
The sole purpose of industrial training is to build in students certain skills and ethics that would make them employable or grant them entrepreneur capacity.

2 How it Works

Industrial Training, also known as on-the-job training, is a valuable opportunity to acquire practical skills and gain industry experience within a specific field. If you have an interest in participating in industrial training, you can follow these steps:

  1. To begin, request an online training completion letter. Once you have completed the online training, you will be able to generate and print out the letter.
  2. The completion letter serves as proof of your training and can be submitted to any organization that offers services related to your chosen course field.
  3. For instance, if you specialize in Responsive Web Design as a student or graduate, you should seek out organizations that provide Responsive Web Design services or closely related services in your preferred location.
  4. Contact the identified organization(s) and express your interest in participating in industrial training with them. Explain that you have completed the necessary online training and attach the printed completion letter to your communication.
  5. Typically, industrial training involves a minimum commitment of 2 to 4 weeks. During this period, you will have the opportunity to work on freelance projects and observe real-world practices within the organization.
    Benefits of Industrial Training:-
  • Practical Skill Acquisition
  • Industry Experience
  • Job Opportunity
